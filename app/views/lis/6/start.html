Apply for help with health costs

The Low Income Scheme

If you find it difficult to pay for health costs you may be entitled to some help towards the cost of:

  • NHS Prescriptions;
  • NHS Dental treatment;
  • NHS wigs and fabric supports;
  • sight tests, glasses and contact lenses;
  • travel to receive NHS treatment if referred by a doctor (GP or hospital doctor), dentist or optician

You can apply at any time. Do not wait until you need treatment.

You cannot get help with health costs if you or your partner (or both) have more than:

  • £16,000 in savings, investments or properties (not counting the place where you live); or
  • £23,250 in savings, investments or properties if you permanently live in a care home

You may already be entitled to help with health costs if you are:

  • getting income support
  • getting pension credit guarantee credit
  • getting universal credit
  • named on, or entitled to, an NHS Tax Credit Exemption certificate

What to do if the above applies to you

You can claim for:

  • yourself;
  • your partner; or
  • your children;
